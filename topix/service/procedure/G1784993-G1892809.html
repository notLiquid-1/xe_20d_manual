Side Panel Sheet Metal Repairs

Rocker Panel (G1784993)

REMOVAL AND INSTALLATION

Loading...

Removal
NOTE:

In this procedure the rocker panel is installed in conjunction with:

  • Front bumper
  • Front fender
  • Front fender splash shield
  • Front door
  • Rear door
  • Front seat
  • Rear seat cushion
  • Cowl side trim panel
  • B-pillar lower trim panel
  • Front scuff plate trim panel
  • Rear scuff plate trim panel
  • Front safety belt retractor
  • Rocker panel moulding
  1. The rocker panel is serviced as indicated.
  2. Before commencing this procedure make sure that you are aware of all Health and Safety requirements.For additional information, refer to: Health and Safety Precautions (100-00, Description and Operation).
  3. For further information on the methods, tools and fixings used in this procedure refer to the body repairs - general information section.
    4. NOTES:

    • Where breakstem fasteners (BSF) removal is instructed remove fastener shaft with a 4mm punch and remove the head with a 6.5mm drill bit. Clean all the drilled holes to make sure they are free from debris.

    • Where self piercing rivets (SPR's) removal is instructed use either the approved SPR tool or 5.3mm drill bit.

    • Any joints sealed as part of vehicle manufacture must be sealed as part of the repair.

  4. For additional information, refer to: Front Bumper (501-19, Removal and Installation).
  5. For additional information, refer to: Fender (501-02, Removal and Installation).
  6. For additional information, refer to: Fender Splash Shield (501-02, Removal and Installation).
  7. For additional information, refer to: Front Door (501-03, Removal and Installation).
  8. For additional information, refer to: Rear Door (501-03, Removal and Installation).
  9. For additional information, refer to: Front Row Seat (501-10, Removal and Installation).
  10. For additional information, refer to: Rear Seat Cushion (501-10, Removal and Installation).
  11. For additional information, refer to: Cowl Side Trim Panel (501-05, Removal and Installation).
  12. For additional information, refer to: B-Pillar Lower Trim Panel (501-05, Removal and Installation).
  13. For additional information, refer to: Front Scuff Plate Trim Panel (501-05, Removal and Installation).
  14. For additional information, refer to: Rear Scuff Plate Trim Panel (501-05, Removal and Installation).
  15. For additional information, refer to: Front Safety Belt Retractor (501-20A, Removal and Installation).
  16. For additional information, refer to: Rocker Panel Moulding (501-08, Removal and Installation).
  17. Release and Position the floor carpet to one side.
  18. Release and position the floor noise, vibration and harshness (NVH) material to one side.
  19. Release and position the bulkhead NVH material to one side.
  20. Release and position the wiring harness to one side.
  21. Remove the front door lower door hinge from the A-pillar.
  22. Remove the rear door lower door hinge from the B-pillar.
  23. CAUTION:

    Care should be taken not to cut through into the inner reinforcements.

    Using a combination of the new rocker panel and the measurements mark out the position where the metal inert gas (MIG) butt joints are to be made. Cut through the panel as indicated.
  24. Remove the SPR's as indicated.
  25. Remove the SPR's as indicated.
  26. NOTE:

    Avoid where possible damaging the NVH components located behind the rocker panel.

    Carefully separate the joints and remove the rocker panel also releasing the NVH components as indicated.
Installation
CAUTION:

Where structural adhesive is to be applied between surfaces it is essential compression is applied to the joints during the curing period.

NOTES:

  • BSF installation involves using the Jaguar approved BSF installation tool and requires a hole diameter of 6.5mm.

  • SPR installation requires using the Jaguar approved SPR tool.

  1. Remove the rivet remnants.
  2. Dress flanges where necessary.
  3. Offer up the new rocker panel and clamp into position. Check alignment if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  4. Drill holes where the BSF's are to be installed as indicated.
  5. Drill holes where the BSF's are to be installed as indicated.
  6. Drill holes where the backing plates are to be installed as indicated.
  7. Drill holes in the new rocker panel where the backing plates are to be installed as indicated.
  8. Remove the new rocker panel.
  9. Deburr the drilled holes.
  10. Measure and cut three 50mm wide backing plates, 25mm each side of the MIG butt joint from the rocker panel remnant.
  11. Cut six run on/run off tabs from the rocker panel remnant.
  12. Using a fine bristle disc clean and prepare the panel surfaces including the NVH components.
  13. Install and MIG plug weld the backing plates as indicated.
  14. Apply the coupling agent where the Jaguar recommended bonding material is to be applied and allow to dry.
  15. NOTE:

    Make sure a continuous bead of adhesive surrounds fixing holes. Do not apply adhesive in the vicinity of the MIG welds.

    Apply a 5mm zig zag bead of the Jaguar recommended bonding material to the rocker panel and the NVH components as indicated.
  16. Offer up the new rocker panel and clamp into position. Check alignment if correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  17. Tack MIG weld the butt joints as indicated.
  18. MIG plug weld the backing plates as indicated.
  19. Install the BSF's as indicated.
  20. Install the BSF's as indicated.
  21. Install the SPR's as indicated.
  22. Install the SPR's as indicated.
  23. MIG weld the butt joints as indicated.
  24. Dress all welds.
  25. Remove any excess adhesive.
  26. Cut off the run on/run off tabs.
  27. Carry out a non destructive crack inspection on the MIG butt joints. If correct proceed to next step, if not rectify and recheck before proceeding.
  28. Install the front door lower door hinge and tighten the bolts to 30Nm.
  29. Install the rear door lower door hinge and tighten the bolts to 30Nm.
  30. Make sure that any open or exposed panel joints are correctly sealed.
  31. Make sure that all installed SPR's are sealed using a Jaguar recommended sealer.
  32. Make sure corrosion protection is applied to all areas affected by repair.
  33. The installation of associated panels and components is the reversal of removal procedure.
