NOTES:

  • Only use calibrated equipment recommended by Jaguar Land Rover.

  • A change in camber means a change in toe, therefore camber must always be adjusted first (only where adjustable).

  • Some variation in the illustrations may occur, but the essential information is always correct.

  • Some components shown removed for clarity.

  1. NOTE:

    Vehicles with air suspension only.

    Set the vehicle to tight tolerance mode before taking any geometry readings or making any geometry adjustments.
  2. Note the correct geometry values before carrying out this procedure.
  3. Check the tie rod ends, suspension joints, wheel bearings and wheels and tires for damage, wear and free play.
  4. Roll the vehicle backwards and forwards to settle the steering and suspension.
  5. Align the steering wheel to straight ahead.
    6. NOTE:

    Carry out Steps 6 to 9 only if the rear subframe has been removed.

  6. Loosen, but do not fully remove the bolts.
  7. Adjust the wheel base. Note the readings from the steering geometry test equipment and adjust the geometry as required.
  8. Adjust the axle set-back. Note the readings from the steering geometry test equipment and adjust the geometry as required.
  9. Adjust the axle lateral off-set. Note the readings from the steering geometry test equipment and adjust the geometry as required.
  10. Tighten the 4 subframe to body bolts to the specified torque.
      • TORQUE: 170 Nm
  11. NOTE:

    Both toe links must be adjusted by an equal amount.

    Adjust the rear wheel toe alignment. Note the readings from the steering geometry test equipment and adjust the geometry as required.
      • Stage 1:60 Nm
      • Stage 2: 90°
  12. CAUTIONS:

    • To prevent damage to the tie rods, use an additional wrench when loosening or tightening the components.

    • Do not allow the gaiter to twist.

    NOTES:

    • Both tie rods must be rotated by an equal amount.

    • Repeat the procedure for the other side.

    Adjust the front wheel toe alignment. Note the readings from the steering geometry test equipment and adjust the geometry as required.
      • TORQUE: 100 Nm
  13. Check, and if necessary, repeat the wheel alignment procedure until the correct values are obtained.
  14. Using Jaguar Land Rover approved diagnostic system, calibrate the steering angle sensor.
