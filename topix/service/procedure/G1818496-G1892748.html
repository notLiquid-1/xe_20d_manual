Removal
WARNINGS:

  • Always wear safety glasses when repairing an airbag Supplementary Restraint System (SRS) vehicle and when handling an airbag.

  • Never probe the connectors on the airbag. Doing so may result in airbag deployment. Failure to follow this instruction may result in personal injury.

  • To avoid accidental deployment and possible personal injury, the backup power supply must be depleted before repairing or replacing any airbag Supplementary Restraint System (SRS) components. To deplete the backup power supply energy, disconnect the startup battery ground cable and wait one minute. Failure to follow this instruction may result in personal injury.

  • Make sure that the airbag wiring harness is routed correctly, failure to follow this instruction could cause a faulty airbag.

NOTE:

This procedure shows North American Specification (NAS) and non NAS varriants.

All vehicles

  1. Make sure the Supplementary Restraint System (SRS) is safe.
    Refer to: Supplementary Restraint System Health and Safety Precautions (100-00 General Information, Description and Operation).
  2. Disconnect the startup battery ground cable.
    Refer to: Startup Battery Disconnect and Connect (414-01 Battery, Mounting and Cables, General Procedures).
  3. Rotate the steering wheel.
  4. NOTE:

    Gentle pressure applied to the airbag towards the instrument panel aids release.

    Release the clip.
  5. NOTE:

    Gentle pressure applied to the airbag towards the instrument panel aids release.

    Release the clip.
  6. Rotate the steering wheel.

Non NAS vehicles

  1. CAUTION:

    Take extra care not to damage the wiring harnesses.

    NOTE:

    Make a note of the orientation of the wiring harness to aid installation.

    Disconnect the electrical connectors and remove the driver airbag.

NAS vehicles

  1. CAUTION:

    Take extra care not to damage the wiring harnesses.

    NOTE:

    Make a note of the orientation of the wiring harness to aid installation.

    Disconnect the electrical connectors and remove the driver airbag.
Installation

Non NAS vehicles

  1. WARNING:

    Make sure the driver airbag wiring harness is installed to the retaining clips and located as highlighted in the illustration.

    CAUTION:

    Take care not to damage the driver airbag wiring harness.

    • Connect the electrical connector.
    • Install the driver airbag harness to the driver airbag.

NAS vehicles

  1. WARNING:

    Make sure the driver airbag wiring harness is installed to the retaining clips and located as highlighted in the illustration.

    CAUTION:

    Take care not to damage the airbag wiring harness.

    • Connect the electrical connector.
    • Install the driver airbag harness to the driver airbag.

All vehicles

  1. WARNING:

    Driver airbag installation can be confirmed by hearing 2 audible clicks, 1 for each clip.

    CAUTION:

    Take extra care not to damage the wiring harnesses.

    Install the driver airbag.
  2. Connect the startup battery ground cable.
    Refer to: Startup Battery Disconnect and Connect (414-01 Battery, Mounting and Cables, General Procedures).
  3. If a new component has been installed, configure using Jaguar Land Rover approved diagnostic equipment.
    General Equipment: Jaguar Land Rover diagnostic equipment
